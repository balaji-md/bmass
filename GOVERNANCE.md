# Governance

## Purpose

BMASS is an open-source research project exploring new approaches to human-centred computing.

This document describes how decisions are made, how contributors become maintainers, and how the project evolves.

Our goal is to remain open to ideas while maintaining technical quality, security, and a coherent architectural vision.

---

# Guiding Principles

BMASS is guided by the following principles:

- Open collaboration
- Technical excellence
- Scientific curiosity
- Human oversight
- Security by design
- Privacy by default
- Transparency
- Respectful discussion

Ideas are judged on their technical merit rather than the seniority, background, or affiliation of the contributor.

---

# Project Leadership

BMASS is currently led by the project founder and maintainer.

The maintainer is responsible for:

- Defining the long-term architectural direction.
- Reviewing and merging pull requests.
- Managing releases.
- Coordinating community contributions.
- Ensuring consistency with the project's objectives.
- Protecting the integrity and security of the codebase.

As the project grows, additional maintainers may be appointed.

---

# Decision Making

Technical decisions are made through open discussion whenever practical.

When consensus is reached, it is preferred.

When consensus cannot be reached, the project maintainer will make the final decision after considering:

- Technical correctness
- Simplicity
- Maintainability
- Security
- Long-term sustainability
- Alignment with the project's vision

Disagreement is expected and encouraged. Constructive debate strengthens the project.

---

# Becoming a Maintainer

Maintainers are selected based on sustained contributions rather than titles or credentials.

Factors considered include:

- Consistent, high-quality contributions
- Technical expertise
- Constructive participation in discussions
- Respectful collaboration
- Commitment to the project's long-term goals
- Sound engineering judgement

There is no fixed timeline or contribution threshold.

Maintainers are invited by the project leadership when appropriate.

---

# Pull Requests

All contributions are welcome.

Pull requests should:

- Address a clearly defined problem.
- Be focused in scope.
- Include appropriate documentation where necessary.
- Preserve existing functionality unless intentionally changing behaviour.
- Be open to review and discussion.

Large architectural changes should be discussed before implementation.

---

# Issues and Discussions

Issues should be used for:

- Bugs
- Feature requests
- Documentation improvements
- Technical tasks

Discussions are encouraged for:

- Research ideas
- Architectural proposals
- Long-term vision
- Questions
- Experimental concepts

---

# Releases

Releases are made when significant milestones are achieved rather than on a fixed schedule.

Experimental features may remain in development until considered sufficiently mature.

Stability takes precedence over release frequency.

---

# Security

Security concerns take priority over new functionality.

Where necessary, security-related pull requests may receive expedited review.

Known vulnerabilities may delay releases until resolved.

---

# Community

BMASS welcomes contributors from many disciplines, including:

- Operating systems
- Artificial intelligence
- Linux
- Embedded systems
- Robotics
- Cybersecurity
- Healthcare
- Research
- Education
- Technical writing

Diversity of expertise strengthens the project.

---

# Project Evolution

BMASS is an experimental platform.

Its architecture will evolve as new evidence, testing, and engineering experience become available.

No design decision is considered beyond question.

Good ideas survive scrutiny.

Better ideas replace them.

---

# Long-Term Stewardship

The objective of BMASS is to build an enduring open-source project that remains useful, transparent, and community-driven.

As the community grows, governance may evolve toward a broader maintainer model while preserving the project's core principles of openness, safety, and technical excellence.

Changes to this governance document will be discussed openly and documented in the project's history.

---

# Funding and Sponsorship

BMASS is an open-source research project.

Financial support helps accelerate development, testing, documentation, infrastructure, community engagement, and long-term sustainability.

## Principles

Funding decisions will be guided by:

- Transparency
- Accountability
- Fairness
- Sustainability
- Advancement of the project

Sponsors do not receive control over the project's technical direction.

Technical decisions remain independent.

---

## How Funds May Be Used

Sponsorship may be used for:

- Hardware
- Model training
- Infrastructure and hosting
- Development time
- Documentation
- Testing and benchmarking
- Security reviews
- Conference presentations
- Community events
- Educational material
- Administrative costs

---

## Contributors

Open-source contributions are voluntary.

Submitting code or documentation does not automatically create a financial entitlement.

Where funding permits, the project may support contributors through:

- Grants
- Bounties
- Commissioned work
- Honoraria
- Reimbursement of approved project expenses

Any such arrangements will be communicated transparently.

---

## Financial Transparency

The project aims to provide periodic summaries of sponsorship received and broad categories of expenditure.

As the project grows, additional governance mechanisms may be introduced.

---

## Future Governance

If BMASS reaches a scale where significant funding is received, a formal governance and financial framework may be established.

Possible future models include:

- Open Collective
- Non-profit foundation
- Community steering committee
- Independent advisory board

The funding model will evolve with the needs of the project while preserving its open-source principles.

---

Thank you to every sponsor, contributor, and supporter who helps advance BMASS.
