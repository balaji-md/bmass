# Vision

BMASS explores a simple question.

**What happens when the language model becomes a native part of the computing environment rather than just another application?**

This project investigates that question through practical engineering.

Rather than replacing conventional operating systems, BMASS currently builds upon them, progressively shifting more interaction toward a model-native architecture while retaining deterministic operating system services underneath.

The long-term research direction is a computing environment where probabilistic reasoning and deterministic execution work together under continuous human supervision.

We describe this architecture as:

## PDHITL

**Probabilistic Deterministic Human-In-The-Loop Computing**

The model reasons.

The operating system executes.

The human decides.

# Why BMASS?

Artificial intelligence is becoming one of the defining technologies of our generation.

The question is not whether AI will become more capable.

- local
- private
- transparent
- affordable
- inspectable
- culturally adaptable

Rather than competing directly with large cloud models, BMASS aims to complement them by enabling entirely new classes of local intelligent systems.

## Phase 1 — Foundation ✔

## Phase 2 — Model-Native Computing

Current development

Focus areas include:

- richer system interaction
- improved hardware awareness
- persistent memory
- enhanced tool orchestration
- secure local knowledge integration
- improved user workflows

Objective

Transform BMASS from an experimental interface into a practical daily computing environment.

---

## Phase 3 — Personalised Local Intelligence

Research and development

Areas of investigation include:

- local adaptation
- efficient fine-tuning workflows
- organisation-specific knowledge
- secure local model optimisation
- evaluation and rollback mechanisms

Objective

Enable systems to become increasingly useful for their owners while remaining transparent and locally controlled.

---

## Phase 4 — Safety and Integrity

Research

Future work will investigate methods for:

- behavioural evaluation
- safety monitoring
- integrity verification
- execution boundaries
- human approval workflows
- recovery mechanisms
- reproducible validation

Objective

Study how adaptive systems can remain dependable over long periods of operation.

---

## Phase 5 — PDHITL Computing

Research

Develop computing environments where:

- models perform probabilistic reasoning
- deterministic software performs execution
- humans retain authority over important decisions

The goal is not autonomous replacement of people.

The goal is amplified human capability.

---

## Phase 6 — Distributed Model Collaboration

Long-term research

Potential areas include:

- peer-to-peer collaboration
- privacy-preserving optimisation
- distributed evaluation
- secure model exchange
- community-owned infrastructure

This research will prioritise privacy, security, consent and transparency.

Whether distributed learning proves practical remains an open research question that BMASS hopes to explore collaboratively with the wider community.
